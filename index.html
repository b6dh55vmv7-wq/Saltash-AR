About Saltash

Saltash is a historic market town located in Cornwall, England, situated on the west bank of the River Tamar. With roots dating back to medieval times, Saltash has played a significant role in British maritime history and industrial development.

The town is most famous for the Royal Albert Bridge, an iconic Victorian engineering marvel designed by Isambard Kingdom Brunel. Completed in 1859, this wrought iron railway bridge spans the River Tamar and remains one of the most recognizable landmarks in Cornwall.

Saltash's strategic location made it an important port for the export of tin and copper during the industrial era. The town's maritime heritage is deeply connected to the Age of Exploration, with connections to notable figures such as Sir Francis Drake.

Today, Saltash continues to be a vibrant community that celebrates its rich history while embracing modern technology. This AR tour is designed to help visitors and residents alike discover and appreciate the historical significance of this remarkable Cornish town.
