How to Use AR Markers

The Saltash WebAR Tour uses standard AR markers to trigger the augmented reality experience. Each marker corresponds to a different historical landmark in Saltash. To use the tour, you'll need to have these markers available—either printed on paper or displayed on a screen.

Below are the three markers used in the tour. You can download them, print them at full size (recommended 10cm × 10cm or larger), and then point your smartphone camera at them while using the AR tour to see the interactive 3D models and historical information.

📋 Setup Instructions

1. Download Markers

Click the download button on each marker card to save the marker images to your computer.

2. Print Markers

Print the markers on standard paper. Recommended size: 10cm × 10cm or larger. Ensure the printout is clear and not distorted.

3. Open AR Tour

Visit the AR Tour page on your smartphone and allow camera access.

4. Point & Explore

Point your smartphone camera at the printed markers. The AR experience will activate, showing 3D models and historical information.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • Lighting: Use good lighting conditions. Avoid direct sunlight or very dark environments.
  • Marker Size: Larger markers (15cm × 15cm or more) work better for detection.
  • Distance: Hold your device 20-50cm away from the marker for optimal detection.
  • Angle: Point your camera directly at the marker, not at an extreme angle.
  • Quality: Print markers on high-quality paper for better recognition.
  • Device: Use a smartphone or tablet with a good camera for the best experience.
